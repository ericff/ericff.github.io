A Working Paper on Optimization Methods for a Particular Model

This paper examines issues with search methods to estimate the parameters of the heteroskedastic probit model, a generalization of the well-known probit model. We document problems with the search methods on simulated and real data, and give an intuitive explanation for why we think the failures occur. We also suggest some ideas to correct the issues.

Working Paper

Graphs of U.S. Labor Force Participation Rates, for Subgroups of the Population

This is a small project I wrote in R to download and create multiple graphs of Labor Force Participation Rates of various subgroups of the U.S. population. I was interested in understanding the overall Participation Rate, and I thought it would be interesting to look at subgroups of the population.

Tools for Change in STEM Cost Simulator

Working with data from a University of California faculty survey and UC Berkeley Professor Clair Brown, and others at The Center for WorkLife Law, UC Hastings College of the Law, I designed the back end of a web tool, which uses regression and probit regression analysis to estimate the effect of family-friendly policies on cost savings through retention of faculty. The project worked towards retaining women in the STEM faculty pipeline.
